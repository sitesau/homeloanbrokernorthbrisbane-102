Typical costs and fees

Borrowing costs usually include interest, application fees, valuation charges and sometimes lenders mortgage insurance. Not every cost is obvious upfront, so ask for an itemised list of fees before committing.

Comparing the comparison rate rather than the headline rate gives a clearer picture of total cost over the first years of the loan.

Choosing who to work with

A broker can compare products from multiple lenders, while a bank only offers its own products. Both routes have their place. What matters is finding someone who explains the options clearly and discloses commissions.

Check licensing on the ASIC professional registers and read any reviews or complaints that are publicly available.

Common mistakes to avoid

Skipping the pre-approval step is a frequent misstep. It leaves you unsure about your budget and can weaken your negotiating position. Another is accepting the first rate without comparing at least two other options.

Also watch for loans with heavy exit fees if you plan to refinance within the first few years.

  1. Set your budget. Factor in repayments, fees and a buffer for rate changes.
  2. Compare products. Look at comparison rate, features and exit fees side by side.
  3. Prepare documents. Have income, expenses, ID and property details ready.
  4. Apply for pre-approval. A conditional approval strengthens your offer.

Common questions

How long does pre-approval take? Most lenders return a conditional pre-approval within two to four business days if the documents are complete.

What is the difference between a broker and a bank lender? A broker can compare products from multiple lenders; a bank lender only offers that bank's own products.

Do I need a 20% deposit? No, but a deposit below 20% usually means paying lenders mortgage insurance.
